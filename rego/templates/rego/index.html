Why Adobe Illustrator?

Adobe Illustrator is a production-standard software for graphic design.
Unlike MS paint or photoshop, it is a vector-oriented (rather than pixel-oriented) graphics design tool. This means that the graphic is made up of shapes, rather than pixels. This lends itself very well to designing sharp, precise graphic in a short length of time!

However, the toolkit featured in Illustrator is not very intuitive for people coming from simpler graphics programs. The purpose of this workshop is to give people a quick run-down on the most useful tools in Illustrator, and how to use them to get the result you are looking for.

With a bit of practise, you will find that you can draw much faster than you would have in Paint or Powerpoint, and with the potential to create much more impressive figures, diagrams and pictures. You can even use it to touch-up graphs and charts that have been exported from other programs. See below for examples.

And best of all, USC has made the full Adobe Suite available for everyone to download. If it's not already in your software center, please contact IT.

In the workshop we will cover:

  • Setting up your workspace
  • Shapes, strokes and fills
  • The pen tool: draw and modify shapes
  • The select tools: for selecting objects (of course)
  • The shape-builder tool: for creating complex shapes from simple ones
  • Image-trace: turn pixel-images into shapes
  • Layers and how to use them effectively
  • Transparency and blending
  • Gradients
  • Effects and filters
  • Saving and exporting

The workshop will be held in a computing lab with the appropriate software installed already, so just bring yourself and a notepad.
